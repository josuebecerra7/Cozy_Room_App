About

An efficient and easy to use website for monitoring of room comfort level based on the parameters such as temperature, sound and light. The air quality of the room is also calculated with emulation of variables such as carbon dioxide (Co2), nitrogen dioxide (No2) and particulate matter 10 (PM10). This website also provides an option of retrieving all the previous data through API. The Heat Index is a measure of how hot it really feels when relative humidity is factored in with the actual air temperature. It also includes parameters for average values of the skin's exposure to the sun, which will increase its temperature in relation to the air, and winds that help the evaporation of sweat and therefore the cooling of the skin. Exposure to full sunshine can increase heat index values. Also, strong winds, particularly with very hot, dry air, can be extremely hazardous.

An air quality index is a measure of air quality, allowing different data to be synthesized into a single value. It is defined by pollutant, depending on the quantity and type of this pollutant, considering parameters like CO2, NO2, PM10, etc. It applies to indoor air (public or private), to the air in the workplace and / or to the outdoor ambient air.
